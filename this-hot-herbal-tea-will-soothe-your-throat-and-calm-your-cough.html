This Hot Herbal Tea Will Soothe Your Throat and Calm Your Cough

jesse martin May 21, 2023

You know the feeling: One minute, you're happily tapping away on a TPS report at work, and the next, you're suddenly not feeling so hot. Your nose is running, you're a little tired, and…uh oh. There it is: a cough. Suddenly, your week is shot.

As soon as the temperature drops, it seems like we're constantly battling hacking coughs and tickles in our throats. Over-the-counter medicines may knock us out for the night, but let's be honest: They definitely don't taste great. So this year we're turning to a new way to calm our sore throats—with a homemade hot herbal tonic. Bon Appétit test-kitchen manager Brad Leone created the recipe (he's been known to dabble in tonics), and he's done the research. This recipe not only feels soothing and tastes good—it works. Here's why you should be drinking Brad's cough-suppressant tea.

1. It Has Anti-Inflammatory Properties

Turmeric and oregano are both known for their anti-inflammatory properties, so combining the two will soothe sore and scratchy throats.

2. It's Packed with Vitamin C

Fresh lemon and lime juice add a powerful dose of vitamin C, which will help your immune system kick this cold in the pants—and get you geared up to fight off the next one. Linus Pauling would be so proud!

3. Raw Honey Sweetens the Deal

This tea tastes savory, but a small amount of raw honey keeps it from being too austere. Why use the raw stuff? It's not heat-treated, so the enzymes, antioxidants, and antibacterial properties are kept intact. Of course, this means keeping a careful eye on the tea while it cooks—an accidental boil will kill the good stuff. (One more reason to heat it only to 130 degrees? A boil will turn it brown and muddy-looking.)

4. It'll Get Things Moving

Black peppercorns add some kick and also stimulate mucus flow. Gross, yeah, but also necessary. The sooner you get that moving out of your system, the sooner you'll feel better.

5. It's Soothing, Comforting, and Tastes Great

This isn't your average medicinal cure, taken in a shot and choked down. Thanks to a healthy dose of ginger, it actually tastes good. A little almond milk adds body and texture to the tea (as well as, bonus, an immune-system boost). The herbal notes—thyme and oregano—are vegetal and cooling, the black pepper gives it zing, and the citrus brightens it all. Bottoms up!

Be careful not to boil the tea. It will give you a cleaner flavor and be more nutrient-rich.

Ingredients

4 servings (makes about 8 cups)

1

bunch oregano

1

bunch thyme

1

1” piece ginger, peeled, grated

1

1” piece turmeric, peeled, grated

½

teaspoon black peppercorns

1

small garlic clove, grated

¼

cup honey, preferably raw

¼

cup unsweetened almond milk

3

tablespoons fresh lime or lemon juice

Preparation

  1. Lightly whack oregano and thyme with a wooden spoon to bruise and release oils. Heat herbs, ginger, turmeric, peppercorns, and 8 cups water in a medium saucepan until an instant-read thermometer registers around 130°—it should be uncomfortably hot to the touch (do not let it boil). Reduce temperature to low and let mixture steep 6 minutes. Add garlic, honey, almond milk, and citrus juice, stirring to dissolve honey. Strain into a pitcher or large heatproof container, pushing on the herbs to squeeze out as much liquid as possible. Divide among mugs.
